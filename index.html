GENDER EQUALITY (SDG 5)

Achieving gender equality and empowering all women and girls worldwide.

KEY TARGETS OF SDG 5

  1. End All Forms of Discrimination – Ensure equal rights and eliminate gender-based discrimination globally.
  2. Eliminate Violence Against Women – End violence, exploitation, and harmful practices such as child marriage and FGM.
  3. Recognize and Value Unpaid Care Work – Promote shared domestic responsibilities and support women’s economic empowerment.
  4. Ensure Full Participation in Leadership – Promote equal opportunities for women in political and economic decision-making.
  5. Universal Access to Sexual and Reproductive Rights – Ensure access to healthcare, family planning, and reproductive health services.
  6. Adopt Policies for Gender Equality – Implement legal frameworks to promote equal rights for all genders.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Gender inequality persists in many parts of the world, limiting opportunities for women and girls. Issues like wage gaps, gender-based violence, lack of access to education, and restricted reproductive rights create barriers to equality. Achieving SDG 5 requires strong policies, education, and global cooperation to empower women and ensure equal opportunities for all.

SOLUTIONS

Equal Pay and Economic Empowerment - Enforce laws ensuring equal pay and employment rights for women.


Access to Education - Provide scholarships and incentives for girls’ education worldwide.


Ending Gender-Based Violence - Strengthen laws and support services for victims of gender violence.


Women's Leadership and Political Representation - Encourage women’s participation in politics and leadership roles.
